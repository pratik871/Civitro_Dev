Part 1Platform Identity, Vision & Design Principles

1.1 Platform Identity

Civitro
Platform Name
"Democracy you can see"
Tagline
Citizen-Government Collaboration
Classification

India Governance Chain (Example)

Every verified citizen is automatically connected to all their elected representatives across every level of government, based on home address:
Citizen → Ward Councillor / Mayor → MLA → State Minister → Chief Minister → MP → Cabinet Minister → Prime Minister

The system maps this complete chain automatically — the citizen never needs to know which department or leader handles their issue.

Strategic framing (critical): Civitro is positioned as a collaboration and civic utility platform — NOT a leader rating app or political criticism tool. This framing distinction is the difference between national adoption and being banned within six months. Every public-facing surface — app store listing, onboarding, press materials, government outreach — must reinforce the collaborative, constructive identity.

Vision Statements

  • "Every voice deserves to be heard" — foundational mission
  • "Democracy you can see" — citizen-facing tagline
  • "Transparent 360° feedback" — enabling transparent feedback between citizens and government at every level
  • "India's Civic Nervous System" — long-term systems vision (millions of citizens as distributed sensors for civic infrastructure)
  • "Governance Operating System" — platform evolution target (the three pillars of transparency, accountability, and measurement become the operating layer for democratic governance)
  • "Structural intelligence for democracy" — the platform evolves from complaint handling to governance intelligence network

The Data Layer Vision

The real value of the platform is not the complaint flow — it is the governance data layer that emerges. Over time, Civitro becomes the largest civic dataset in the country. That dataset powers: policy research, urban planning, disaster prediction and response, corruption detection, infrastructure investment decisions, and national policy insights.

1.2 Six User Personas

1 Citizens

  • Post voices (500 chars, media, hashtags, mentions, 15-language translations)
  • Report civic issues (photo + GPS + AI classification in under 30 seconds)
  • Rate elected representatives at ZIP-code/ward granularity
  • Join polls and budget prioritization votes (participatory budgeting)
  • Build civic reputation: Credibility Score → Community Validator → Thought Leader → People's Champion (100K+ followers, community-voted)
  • Direct message verified users and leaders
  • View constituency health, leader performance, promise tracking
  • Premium tier: tagged voices, extended DM history, enhanced discovery, ad-free

2 Elected Leaders

  • Manage constituency through dedicated dashboard with incoming issue queue
  • Receive, triage, and assign citizen-reported issues to staff members
  • Run targeted constituency polls (sentiment, budget prioritization)
  • View heatmap analytics, demographic reports (Datamine), citizen sentiment
  • Respond publicly to issues and voices; track own performance metrics
  • Campaign management: scheduled voices, constituency targeting, engagement tracking
  • Subscription-based access to advanced analytics

3 Political Parties

  • Build and manage organizational charts (national → state → district → booth hierarchy)
  • Manage functionaries (add, remove, assign roles, permission levels)
  • Broadcast to all party functionaries
  • Access party-wide analytics rollup across all party leaders
  • Party identity toggle: users switch between personal and party identity at login
  • Demographic data access across party-controlled constituencies
  • Party subscription billing (monthly/annual tiers)

4 NGOs / RWAs / Clubs

  • Community group creation and member management with onboarding workflows
  • Community broadcast messaging and community-scoped polling
  • Issue tracking within geographic or thematic scope
  • Affordable group subscription plans with bulk engagement tools

5 Advertisers / Brands

  • Create hyper-targeted political and civic advertising campaigns
  • Audience targeting by geography, demographics, interests, political engagement level
  • Full campaign analytics: impressions, clicks, reach, engagement; CPM/CPC billing
  • Self-serve campaign creation dashboard; compliance with political ad regulations

6 Civitro Admin Staff

  • Dedicated internal admin portal (not public-facing) with complete audit trails
  • User and voice suspension; leader profile dispute resolution
  • Advertiser account creation; exit poll configuration and targeting
  • Content moderation queue (AI-flagged + human review); platform health monitoring
  • Transparency report generation; brigading and abuse signal dashboards

1.3 Design Principles

1
Microservices-first

Every bounded context is an independently deployable service with its own data store, scaling profile, and deployment lifecycle.

2
Event-driven

All cross-service communication flows through a central event bus. Services react to domain events, enabling loose coupling and full auditability.

3
Mobile-first

Primary interface is a mobile app. Every feature must work on low-bandwidth Indian networks with offline-capable reporting.

4
AI-native

Classification, sentiment, promise extraction, fraud detection, and CHI computation are core architectural concerns with dedicated ML pipeline infrastructure.

5
Geospatial-first

Location is the fundamental organizing principle. Every user, issue, representative, constituency, and metric is anchored to geography.

6
Multi-tenant

Six distinct user types with different permission models, feature sets, subscription tiers, and data access levels — all on shared infrastructure.

7
Multi-language

15 Indian languages from day one. Every content surface supports translation. UI, notifications, and AI pipelines are language-aware.

8
India-first, global-ready

Optimized for India's Ward → Municipal → Assembly → Parliamentary → State → National hierarchy, with abstraction layers for any country's governance structure.

9
Trust by design

Every rating algorithm, CHI computation, and moderation decision is explainable and auditable. Platform credibility IS the product.

10
Immutable audit trail

The Public Work Ledger, Promise Tracker, and moderation actions use event-sourced patterns. Nothing is silently deleted. History is permanent institutional memory.

1.4 Product Experience Requirements

These are architectural requirements expressed as user experience outcomes that engineering must deliver:

"30-Second Civic Fix" — A citizen reports an issue in under 30 seconds: open app → tap photo → GPS auto-locks → AI classifies → system routes. Zero knowledge of government departments required.
"Neighborhood Control Panel" — The ward map and dashboard must make citizens feel they have a live control panel for their neighborhood — open/resolved/trending issues, department performance, leader responsiveness — not just a complaint box.
"Things actually change because of this" — Resolution feedback loops are the single most critical retention mechanism. Users must see reports acknowledged, assigned, worked on, and completed with timestamps and responsible parties at every step. Without this, users leave.
"Celebration moments" — When an issue is resolved, the notification must feel like a civic win: before/after photos, share prompts, contribution counts ("You're the 4,822nd citizen report this month"). Confetti, not checkmarks.
"Social proof engine" — Every resolved issue, before/after comparison, and share creates social proof. The viral loop (report → fix → celebrate → share → new user) is the primary growth mechanism.
"Good leaders will want this" — The Leader Dashboard must give high-performing leaders political advantage from participating. It is a public performance dashboard, not a punishment system.
"Data-driven political memory" — Politicians rely on short public memory. The Promise Tracker creates permanent, searchable institutional memory. This is the most politically transformative feature.

Service 1 — Identity & Auth

Registration, Aadhaar/DigiLocker KYC, OTP, sessions, fraud detection

Owned Tables

users (id, phone, aadhaar_hash, verification_level, user_type, status, language_preference) · auth_sessions (device_fingerprint, jwt_hash, ip) · verification_records (type: otp/aadhaar/digilocker/gps, status, provider_ref) · kyc_documents (doc_type, hash, 5yr retention) · devices (fingerprint, platform, push_token)

Events

Pub: user.registered user.verified user.suspended user.address_updated · Sub: mod.user_suspended

Endpoints

POST /auth/register · POST /auth/verify-otp · POST /auth/verify-aadhaar · POST /auth/verify-address · GET /auth/me · POST /auth/refresh

Scale: Stateless horizontal. Redis sessions. 5 OTP/phone/hour limit.

Biometric auth, face match vs Aadhaar photo, eKYC 2.0, social login (international).

Service 2 — Geospatial Mapping

Address→constituency resolution, PostGIS boundaries, representative auto-assignment

India Hierarchy

Nation (1) → State/UT (36) → Parliamentary (543) → Assembly (~4,100) → Municipal (~7,900) → Ward (~250,000+)

Tables

boundaries (id, level, name, parent_id, polygon PostGIS, centroid, population) · address_mappings (user_id, geocoded_point, pin_code, gps_verified, matched_ward/assembly/parliamentary_id, confidence) · representative_assignments (user_id, representative_id, boundary_id, level)

Resolution Algorithm

GPS? → ST_Contains(ward.polygon, point)
PIN? → pre-computed PIN→ward table
Address string? → Geocode API → lat/lng → ST_Contains
None? → Manual State→District→Ward dropdown
Validate: GPS within 5km of Aadhaar address
Assign: traverse hierarchy → find all reps → create assignments

Pub: representatives.assigned · Sub: user.registered, user.address_updated

Scale: Read-heavy. PostGIS spatial indices. Redis cache TTL 24h. Pre-computed PIN lookups.

Global boundary import per country, redistricting auto-update, offline boundary data, What3Words.

Service 7 — Rating & Accountability

Dynamic star ratings from objective signals — ZIP/ward granularity

Rating Formula (publicly documented — Trust Framework 9B)

overall = 0.25 × responsiveness + 0.25 × resolution_speed
        + 0.20 × citizen_satisfaction + 0.15 × sentiment
        + 0.15 × chi_improvement
// 0-5 stars. 90-day rolling window. Min 20 samples. Weights public.

Tables

ratings (representative_id, boundary_id, overall_score, component_scores_json) · rating_components · rating_history (TimescaleDB) · satisfaction_surveys

Pub: rating.recomputed rating.milestone_reached · Scale: Batch daily. Cached in Redis.

Comparative ratings, predictive trajectories, seasonal adjustment, per-component explanation.

Service 8 — AI Classification & Vision (CivitroVision)

Image/text classification, severity estimation, duplicate detection, moderation

Pipeline

Photo + GPS + Text
→ Preprocess (224×224, quality check)
→ Vision Transformer (ViT-B/16) → 12 categories + confidence
  >0.8: auto-classify | 0.6-0.8: uncertain flag | <0.6: manual queue
→ Text NER (locations, departments, urgency)
→ Ensemble (image + text weighted average)
→ Severity (Critical/High/Medium/Low from image+GPS context)
→ Auto-route (category + boundary → department + representative)
→ Duplicate check (image hash + feature vector)

classification_results · model_versions · training_data (citizen-verified ground truth) · duplicate_detections

ML: SageMaker endpoint, auto-scale 2-10. Retrain every 1000 verified samples. A/B shadow testing. Latency: <500ms.

Video classification, depth estimation from shadows, cost estimation, temporal satellite change detection.

Service 9 — AI Sentiment & Trends

Multilingual NLP, emotion detection, trending topics, governance signals

Pipeline

Text (15 languages) → Language detect → Translate (if needed)
→ Sentiment (multilingual BERT): positive/negative/neutral
→ Emotion: anger/frustration/hope/satisfaction/fear
→ Urgency score (0-1)
→ Aggregate per-boundary (hourly)
→ Trending: sliding window velocity (1h/6h/24h)
→ Alert: >20% negative shift in 24h

sentiment_scores · trending_topics · sentiment_aggregations · topic_clusters

Streaming: Kafka consumer, <30s latency. Trending recalculated every 5 min.

Predictive issue detection, early warning systems, sarcasm detection, media monitoring.

Service 10 — Promise Tracker

AI extracts promises from speeches, interviews, manifestos, social media — permanent political memory

Promise Lifecycle

Source (speech/interview/manifesto/social media/news) → AI extraction (Bedrock Claude)
→ Deduplication (NLP similarity) → Human verification queue
→ Promise created: on_track
→ Progress tracking (CHI, reports, budgets, news cross-reference)
→ Status: on_track → delayed (no action after 25% timeline)
         → fulfilled (criteria met) | broken (expired + unmet)

Statuses: Detected On Track Delayed Fulfilled Broken

promises · promise_sources · promise_milestones · promise_evidence (budget allocations, project approvals, construction progress photos, news articles, government orders, citizen reports) · delivery_scores

ML: Bedrock Claude extraction. Crawlers: 6h social, daily news. NLP dedup.

Parliament transcript processing, budget PDF parsing, RTI integration, manifesto auto-import.

Service 11 — Constituency Health Index (CHI) Engine

Central intelligence layer — multi-dimensional health score per constituency. "Changes elections from narratives to metrics."

8 Dimensions

1. Infrastructure

Road quality, buildings, public facilities

2. Sanitation

Garbage, cleanliness, waste management

3. Healthcare

Hospital access, wait times, facility conditions

4. Education

School conditions, teacher availability

5. Public Safety

Crime, lighting, emergency response

6. Water Supply

Availability, quality, leakage complaints

7. Transport

Traffic, public transit, road conditions

8. Air Quality

Pollution data, respiratory health signals

CHI Computation

Per dimension:
  score = weighted_avg(report_density, resolution_rate, avg_resolution_time,
                       citizen_satisfaction, external_data_signals)

Overall CHI = weighted_avg(all dimension scores)  // 0-100
Rank within level (ward, assembly, parliamentary)
Daily recompute at 02:00 IST. Incremental every 6h for high-activity areas.

chi_scores (boundary_id, overall 0-100, dimensions_json, rank) · chi_dimensions (weights, signals) · chi_history (TimescaleDB) · signal_sources (APIs, refresh frequency) · chi_signals · constituency_rankings

Pub: chi.recomputed chi.rank_changed chi.alert_triggered (drop >10pts in 30d)

Satellite imagery (road quality, green cover), IoT sensors (air, water, traffic), government open data APIs, predictive CHI forecasting, disaster prediction (multi-dimension drops signal crisis).

Service 12 — Citizen Reputation

Civic Credibility Score + AI-calculated Influence Score — incentivizes accuracy, penalizes abuse, creates trust tiers

Two Score System

Civic Credibility Score (0-1000): Measures accuracy and trustworthiness of civic contributions. Determines tier and platform privileges.

Influence Score (AI-calculated, 0-100): AI-computed personal impact score based on engagement, follower count, voice reach, validation history, and community impact. High-impact users are promoted to Thought Leader status. Users with 100,000+ followers and Influence Score above 90 are eligible for People's Champion — a community-voted recognition validated by follower polling.

Tier Progression

New Citizen (0-199) → Verified Reporter (200-499) → Community Validator (500-799)
→ Thought Leader (800-949) → People's Champion (950+ AND 100K+ followers AND community vote)

Reputation portability across cities, gamification badges, civic leaderboards, reputation decay for inactive users.

Service 15 — Search, Discovery & Trends

Part 8AI/ML Pipeline Architecture — 6 Pipelines

Pipeline 1: Issue Classification (CivitroVision)

Input: Photo + GPS + Text
→ Image: ViT-B/16 → 12 categories + confidence + severity
→ Text: NER → locations, departments, urgency
→ Ensemble: weighted average image+text
→ Auto-route: category + boundary → department + representative
Training: citizen-verified resolved issues. Retrain every 1000 samples.
Serving: SageMaker auto-scaling 2-10. Latency: <500ms.

Pipeline 2: Sentiment Analysis

Input: Text (15 languages)
→ Language detect (fastText) → Translate (if needed)
→ Multilingual BERT → sentiment + confidence
→ Emotion: anger/frustration/hope/satisfaction/fear
→ Urgency: 0.0-1.0
→ Aggregate per-boundary (hourly)
→ Alert on >20% negative shift in 24h
Streaming via Kafka. Latency: <30 seconds.

Pipeline 3: Promise Tracking

Sources: social media (6h), news (daily), interviews, manifesto PDFs, parliament transcripts
→ LLM extraction (Bedrock Claude): promise_text, category, location, timeline
→ NLP deduplication vs existing promises
→ Human verification for high-value promises
→ Progress estimation: cross-reference CHI, budgets, news, citizen reports

Pipeline 4: CHI Computation

Schedule: Daily 02:00 IST (full). Every 6h (incremental, high-activity)
→ Per constituency, per dimension:
  Gather: citizen reports, resolution rates, response times, external data
→ Normalize per signal (0-1 vs national distribution)
→ Weighted dimension score (0-100)
→ Overall CHI = weighted average of dimensions
→ Rank within level (ward/assembly/parliamentary)
→ Alerts on >10pt drop in 30 days

Pipeline 5: Fraud & Abuse Detection

Real-time (per action):
  Login: device fingerprint, IP geo, VPN detection
  Post: toxicity, spam, duplicate content
  Report: image duplicate, GPS spoof, velocity check
  Vote: coordinated pattern, geographic anomaly
Batch (hourly):
  Account clustering, network analysis, temporal synchronization
Output: risk score (0-1)
  >0.9: block + flag | 0.6-0.9: allow + human review | <0.6: normal

Pipeline 6: Corruption Pattern Detection

Signals:
  • Department resolution patterns (consistently slow?)
  • Geographic neglect (certain wards always ignored?)
  • Budget vs complaints (high spending + persistent complaints?)
  • Contractor patterns (same contractor across issues?)
  • Timeline anomalies (completed suspiciously fast without evidence?)
  • Citizen disputes (high dispute rate after completion claims?)
Output: department efficiency scores (slow departments vs fast departments), geographic neglect signals, repeat infrastructure failure patterns, anomaly alerts
NOTE: Generates signals only — human investigation required before any public output.
Model monitoring & drift detection, automated retraining, SHAP explainability, federated learning, on-device inference for offline classification.

Part 9BPublic Trust & Transparency Framework

This section addresses the explicit architecture requirement for "public trust mechanisms" as a distinct concern, separate from abuse prevention. Trust is earned through transparency, not enforcement.

9B.1 Open Rating Methodology

  • Never for: criticism of leaders, negative ratings, unfavorable CHI scores, critical voices

  • "Transparency canary": quarterly statement confirming no government-directed content removal. Absence implies pressure.

9B.5 Political Neutrality Proof
